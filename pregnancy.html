MORE ON PREGNANCY

Early signs of Pregnancy

Morning sickness: Nausea and vomiting, can occur early,
not just in the morning.
Breast changes: Tenderness, swelling, and darkening of areolas.
Fatigue: Feeling unusually tired due to hormonal changes.
Frequent urination: Increased need to urinate as the
uterus grows.
Food cravings or aversions: Strong cravings or aversions
to certain foods.
Mood swings: Emotional ups and downs due to hormonal changes.
Constipation or bloating: Digestive changes caused by hormones.
Light spotting or implantation bleeding: Slight bleeding
when the egg implants.

Pregnancy week by week

Week 1-4: Fertilization occurs, and the zygote implants
in the uterus.
Week 5-8: Major organs and systems form, and the embryo
becomes a recognizable fetus.
Week 9-12: Rapid fetal growth, distinct facial features,
and reduced risk of miscarriage.
Week 13-16: Refined limbs, movements felt by the mother,
and possible gender identification.
Week 17-20: Lanugo and vernix form, quickening felt by
the mother, and viability potential.
Week 21-24: Senses develop further, age of viability reached.
Week 25-28: Brain and lung development continue,
heightened responses to stimuli.
Week 29-32: Weight gain and limited space in the womb.
Week 33-36: Baby's head moves down, bones fully developed
but skull remains soft.
Week 37-40: Full-term, ready for birth.
Remember, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider
for personalized information and care throughout your pregnancy.

Rib Pain during Pregnancy

During pregnancy, rib pain is a common discomfort experienced
by some women. As the uterus expands to accommodate the
growing baby, it pushes against the diaphragm and ribs,
leading to pressure and pain in the ribcage area.
The hormone relaxin also loosens ligaments, which can
contribute to the discomfort.
Additionally, the expanding breasts can add further
strain to the ribcage.
Rib pain during pregnancy is typically more pronounced
in the later stages as the baby grows larger.
To alleviate this discomfort, pregnant individuals can try
maintaining good posture, using pillows for support while
resting, and engaging in gentle stretching exercises.
However, if the pain becomes severe or is accompanied by
other concerning symptoms, it is essential to consult a
healthcare provider to rule out any underlying issues.

Sleep cycle during Pregnancy

First Trimester:
Fatigue and increased need for sleep due to hormonal changes.
Longer and more frequent naps to cope with increased tiredness.
Second Trimester:
Lessened nausea and discomfort may lead to better sleep
quality for some.
Third Trimester:
Increasing physical discomfort, including back pain and
frequent urination, may disrupt sleep.
Difficulty finding a comfortable sleeping position due to
the growing belly.
Anxiety and anticipation about labor and motherhood can contribute
to sleep disturbances.
Tips for Better Sleep During Pregnancy:
Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
Practice relaxation techniques before bedtime.
Use pregnancy support pillows to find a more comfortable
sleeping position.

Labor and Delivery

Labor is the process of childbirth.
It involves stages: first, second, and third stage.
First Stage of Labor:
Early Labor: The cervix begins to open, and contractions
start, but they are mild and irregular.
Active Labor: Contractions become stronger, longer, and more
regular. The cervix continues to open faster.
Transitional Phase: The cervix fully opens (dilates), and
contractions are intense. It's the final step before pushing.
Second Stage of Labor:
Pushing and Delivery: The baby moves
through the birth canal, and the mother actively pushes to
help the baby be born.
Third Stage of Labor:
Delivery of the Placenta: After the baby is born,
the placenta is delivered as the uterus contracts again.
Pain management techniques are used during labor.
Monitoring and care are provided by healthcare professionals.
Birth can happen in different settings (hospital, birthing center, home).
